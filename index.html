On February 27th and 28th I got a chance to go down to Utah State University to participate in their "HackUSU". This is known as a hackathon which means that a single programmer or a team of programmers takes 24 hours to create a project based on a prompt. My team and I took the prompt "Create something that will save you time or make your life easier." Being new to the Software Engineering program, I wanted to go and see how those more experienced would work on a problem. The idea we came up with was to create a site that gave the user small chunks of information and then a quiz in a format similar to Instagram Reels or TikTok. The way we got the information was by using a Groq API that would take an embedded prompt and return a list of facts based on the topic the user chose. We didn't get as far as we would have liked but, in the end, we had a viable product and plans for the future of the project. BYUI is doing a hackathon in the Fall semester and I am planning on attending that one too!
